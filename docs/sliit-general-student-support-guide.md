## About Us

We are a leading non-state degree awarding institute approved by the University Grants Commission (UGC)
under the Universities Act. We are members of the Association of Commonwealth Universities (ACU), as well as
the International Association of Universities (IAU). We are also the first Sri Lankan institute to be accredited by
the Institution of Engineering & Technology, UK.
SLIIT was established in 1999. We opened our doors to 400 students in Metro Campus in Colombo. Currently,
we offer both undergraduate and postgraduate courses and accommodate over 9000 students, including
international students from various regions in the world. More than 9000 alumni have graduated from our
faculties. We take great pride in producing graduates who make meaningful contributions to their communities
and professions.

## Academic Misconduct Investigation Panel (AMIP)

What is an Academic Misconduct Investigation Panel (AMIP) at SLIIT?
The Academic Misconduct Investigation Panel at SLIIT is a Senate-appointed committee that investigates cases
of academic misconduct, such as cheating, plagiarism, or other dishonest academic behavior. The panel ensures
that each case is handled fairly.
Examples of academic misconduct:

- Cheating during exams or assignments
- Plagiarizing (copying someone else's work without proper citation)
- Helping someone else cheat or plagiarize

Please refer to the Student Rule Book on Courseweb for more details:
https://courseweb.sliit.lk/course/view.php?id=17
Things to remember:

- The panel investigates the case and gives a recommendation.
- The final decision is made by the Senate, based on the panel's recommendation.
- Students are strongly encouraged to always follow academic integrity.
  For more information on the Academic Misconduct Investigation Process, please refer the Student Rule book on
  Courseweb.

## Attendance Sheet - Marking your attendance

Attendance Sheet - Marking your attendance
For a student’s name to appear in the system generated printed attendance sheet, a student should have:

- Successfully complete regular semester registration and/or respective module registration
- Fulfilled fee payment without outstanding fees
- Fulfilled the academic requirements for progression
- Registration status updated and available in the student portal (https://student360.sliit.lk/) and I AM SLIIT app

A student whose name is in the system-generated printed attendance sheet has the right to:

- Access SLIIT designated premises
- Participate in lectures, laboratories, libraries, and other academic activities
- Sit all assessments offered for the registered module, including mid-semester assessments and final examinations

The Faculty Examination System/Unit will permit only the students whose names are listed on this sheet/student
portal to complete any type of assessment.

Why It Matters:

1. Final Confirmation of Registration
   Simply submitting registration forms or making fee payments does not complete the process. The appearance of
   your name on the attendance sheet is the final confirmation that your registration has been processed and
   accepted.
2. No Name, No Exam
   Students whose names are not listed on the attendance sheet or student portal will not be permitted to enter the
   exam hall, regardless of prior coursework or attendance. This is to maintain exam integrity and avoid
   administrative confusion.
3. Avoiding Last-Minute Surprises
   Students are strongly encouraged to check the student portal and Hall allocation well in advance of the exam

date. This allows time to resolve any discrepancies or missing information before it’s too late. 4. Proof of Eligibility
For students, the attendance sheet is more than just a list - it is a guarantee of your right to be examined. It
also serves as a control document for lectures, invigilators and examination unit.

What should students do?

- Double-check your registration status on the Student Portal before the deadline.
- Look for your name on the official attendance sheet at your department office at least a week before mid-term exams.
- Report immediately to the Academic Affairs Service Officer or registration office if your name is missing, even if you think you registered correctly.

No assumptions - check it yourself!
Do not assume registration is complete until your name is officially on the exam attendance sheet. This simple
step protects your right to take exams and ensures a smooth academic process.

## Change of Fields of Specialization/Study

Programmes

Transfers between study programmes/specializations shall be offered subject to the following conditions and
students must submit a filled program transfer form to the Academic Affairs Division within the registration
period (notice will be published on the Course web prior to registration commencement).

- Students should possess the admission requirements for the study programme/specialization to which the transfer is requested.
- Transfer will be granted from the beginning of an academic year.
- In exceptional circumstances, students will be allowed to transfer between study programmes during the academic year, subject to completing any related modules in the selected programme/specialization under the module registration category.
- Balance payments should be arranged by the students (previous semesters), if applicable.
- Program transfers for some specializations (FoC-IM/SE/DS) will be considered by the faculty based on the entry requirements.

### Change of Field of Centers / Weekday-Weekend Status

After the commencement of the semester transfer from the registered center to another will not be permitted
and students must request transfers during the registration period (details will be notified via the Course web
notices).
Weekday/weekend transfers are facilitated based on capacity constraints and as per availability of the programs.

- Registrations for years 1 and 2 FoC / years 3 and 4 SBS will be on a first-come, first-served basis. If the weekend count exceeds the capacity of weekend groups, students who register last will be required to attend the weekday program (MoE IFSLS students cannot register under the weekend category).
- For Year 3 and 4 FoC / Year 2 SBS programmes offered on weekends, students must submit a valid letter of employment or proof of registration for the internship module. Only after documents are reviewed by the Academic Affairs Division before semester commencement will students be permitted to enroll in weekend programmes. Students requesting WD/WE transfer after commencement should contact the faculty (MoE students must register under the internship module to participate in the weekend program).

The Pass grade at SLIIT is “C”.

### What is a conceded pass?

Discontinued from 2022 regular intake onwards. Refer to Academic Progression Criteria on Courseweb.

Conceded Pass (PC) grades are available in some circumstances in undergraduate programmes (Except
programmes offered in Faculty of Humanities and Sciences).
A student cannot request a conceded pass. A conceded pass may only be offered by the institute upon the
recommendation of the Senate. The Institute's Board of Examiners will be responsible for determining the
eligibility and awarding of conceded passes.
A conceded pass will be recorded as a (PC) grade. The numeric marks remain unchanged.  
Conceded passes are not available in postgraduate coursework units as well as for Faculty of Humanities and
Sciences.
Pls refer the Rule book section 3.2.3

### What is a D pass / D criteria?

Discontinued from 2022 regular intake onwards. Refer to Academic Progression Criteria on Courseweb.

To obtain a D pass, all of the below had to be fulfilled:

- Pass the rest of the modules in the 1st attempt
- Only one module pending
- The grade must be `D`, `D+`, or `C-` (`E` and `D-` are not considered)
- GPA must be 2.00 or above
- D criteria is not applicable for English modules
- Not applicable to students enrolled in Faculty of Humanities and Sciences degree programs

The Weighted Grade Point Average is computed by dividing the sum of the products of the number of credits of
all the courses followed and the grade points earned for those courses by a student in a year, by the total
number of credits for the courses followed that year. https://support.sliit.lk/kb/articles/grading-at-sliit
This is then multiplied by an appropriate weighting factor and summed up over the total number of years related
to the specific academic qualification.

WGPA weighting factors by faculty:

- School of Business (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th year): 10%, 20%, 30%, 40%
- Faculty of Computing (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th year): 0%, 20%, 30%, 50%
- Faculty of Engineering (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th year): 10%, 20%, 30%, 40%

This weighted GPA is taken in to consideration when deciding on awarding classes for students of 2017 intake
onwards. Below sample calculations are provided for you to obtain a general idea.

Students enrolled in Undergraduate Degree programs at SLIIT are eligible to obtain level completion certificates

# Lockdown Browser Complete Guide

## Overview

Some exams require that you use the Respondus Lockdown Browser. When using the Lockdown Browser, you will be unable to print, copy, go to another URL, or access other applications. When an assessment is started, you will be locked in until it is submitted for grading.

## Using Lockdown Browser

### Step 1: Installing Lockdown Browser

LockDown Browser must be installed to each computer being used to take a test.

- Download Lockdown Browser
- Follow the onscreen instructions to complete the install

### Step 2: Practice Test

Please attempt the test quiz to check the accessibility to the online examination environment.

Click below button to start quiz: **START PRACTICE TEST**

### Step 3: When Taking an Exam

1. Close all programs, unless one is used to connect you to the Internet
2. Log into exam server, select your course and navigate to the quiz
3. Click the "Launch LockDown Browser" button to open LockDown Browser and start the quiz
4. The test will then start. Once a test has been started with Respondus LockDown Browser, you cannot exit until the Submit button is clicked

**Important:** If the Internet (or wi-fi) connection is lost for an extended period of time, you would not be able to continue with the exam, just as if you were only using LockDown Browser. You need to restart the browser and attempt the exam again.

## Frequently Asked Questions: During a Session

### Font size is too small during LockDown Browser exam

If you are a Windows user, to adjust the font size, click on the "i" icon in the LockDown Browser toolbar. This will open a new window where you can adjust the "zoom level" for your test. Zoom magnifies the entire page, including pictures.

### When I start Respondus LockDown Browser a warning message states "You must close the following program before starting the browser..." Why does this occur?

Many types of applications are blocked from running at the same time as Respondus LockDown Browser. These include screen capture, messaging, screen-sharing, network monitors, and several other types of applications.

### Error codes: -7, -21, -101, -105, -106, -111, -118 and similar 0-199 codes

The error codes typically indicate a problem with the network, not with LockDown Browser. They will often be accompanied by a message that says "Failed to load page. Please check network connection." If the student receives any of these errors, contact support.

### LockDown Browser froze during a test and I cannot exit

When LockDown Browser appears to "freeze", it is commonly due to a dialog box from other applications running in the background appearing underneath the secure LockDown Browser layer or there are corrupted web links, which are blocked.

### I receive a warning: The browser can't be used in virtual machine software such as Virtual PC, VMWare and Parallels

To maintain the academic integrity of an exam, students are not permitted to run LockDown Browser Student Edition when a virtual machine is detected on the system. This includes VM host applications (VMWare, VirtualBox, etc), thin apps (VMWare ThinApp, and others).

## Frequently Asked Questions: With Respondus Monitor

### Spinning Wheel During Webcam Check

If you encounter any of the above "spinning wheel" errors (and blanked out frame where your webcam image should be), it's because another program, background process, or privacy setting on your computer is preventing LockDown Browser from activating your webcam.

### Mac: Webcam Not Detected

If your webcam is not detected at some point during the pre-exam webcam check, and you see an error message, it is likely caused by 3rd party anti-virus software that is installed on your computer. It's also possible that you aren't seeing the expected message.

When there are warnings of not being able to detect the webcam, or you experience a "Spinning Wheel" icon (but no webcam image) we recommend students follow these steps:

1. Please exit the pre-exam webcam check, and click the "i" icon in the LockDown Browser toolbar
2. Refer to additional support resources for detailed troubleshooting

### "Video connection timed out"

If you encounter the "Video connection timed out" error, please do the following:

1. Try clicking the "try again" button on that same page. This may permit the connection to be established.
2. It's only necessary to click this button one time, as doing this multiple times may cause additional delays

### Webcam Blocked

If you see a blocked webcam image during your pre-exam webcam check, it is because of a privacy setting that is enabled in your camera's software utility. Please be aware that this same image will be all that is displayed to your instructor when they review your session.

### What is Prorata Registration?

Prorata registration is the registration granted to a student who has not completed the requirements to proceed
to the next year of study. He/she will have to repeat the respective failed modules and satisfy the required
progression criteria to proceed to the next academic year.

### When will Prorata Registration open?

Prorata registration is usually opened along with the latter part of the regular semester registration. Faculty
based prorata registration notices will be published on Courseweb.

### How to apply for Prorata Registration?

During the registration period, the online prorata registration form will be available in the `http://study.sliit.lk/` portal.

Prorata module fees are applicable:

- For students with regular semester registrations, the applicable registration fee per module is a prorated flat fee and will be published in the relevant Courseweb notice.
- For students without a regular semester registration, the applicable registration fee per module will be published in the relevant Courseweb notice.

CA marks and repeats:

- Students who have obtained pass marks in CA within the validity period will be allowed to carry forward marks under prorata registration and attempt the end-semester examination only.
- CA validity period information will be available on the notice.
- Students who do not have valid CA marks will have to repeat the entire module under prorata registration.

## Semester Performance Based Scholarships

### SLIIT Semester Performance Based Scholarships (From First Year First Semester Onwards)

SLIIT offers full and partial scholarships to undergraduate students based on their academic performance. These scholarships are awarded each semester and aim to support and encourage students who demonstrate exceptional academic excellence. The scholarship is a contribution by SLIIT and is awarded at the discretion of the institute.

### Scholarship Award Criteria

The following criteria will be applied in the selection of recipients for semester performance based scholarships at SLIIT:

- Students must achieve a minimum semester GPA of 3.70
- The minimum class size of the relevant degree program or specialization group must be 100 students
- Eligible students will be ranked according to their semester GPA within their class:
  - The top 1% of students will be considered for a full scholarship
  - The next 2% of students will be considered for a partial scholarship
- In cases where the specialization group has fewer than 100 students, students who have attained an exceptionally high GPA may still be considered for a full or half scholarship, depending on the total number of scholarships awarded across the institute for that semester
- Students enrolled in partner university degree programs at SLIIT will be eligible for these scholarships only up to the SLIIT Diploma level
- The maximum scholarship amount for any semester will be equivalent to the semester fee of the corresponding SLIIT program of which the best performance was shown

These criteria ensure a fair and transparent process that rewards academic excellence.

### Notification of Scholarship Eligibility

Eligible students will be notified of their scholarship status through the following process:

- **Official E-mail Notification:** Each student selected for a scholarship will receive a personalized e-mail from the Registry Officer or the Academic Affairs Division
- **Formal Letter (PDF Attachment):** A formal scholarship award letter may be provided as a PDF attachment in the e-mail for official use or personal records

### Scholarship Fee Adjustments and Refund Procedures for Students

**Deducted from the Semester Fee:** The scholarship amount will be automatically adjusted against the upcoming semester tuition fees. Students can view the updated semester fee reflecting this deduction in the student registration interface when logging in to register for the upcoming semester.

**For Graduated or Already Paid Students:** If students have already paid their semester fees or have graduated, they may request a refund equal to their scholarship amount by contacting the Registry Officer via support.sliit.lk or email the request to studentservices@sliit.lk with a request for a refund.

WES - World Education Assessment : https://www.wes.org/
The WES evaluation converts educational credentials from any country in the world into their U.S.
equivalents. It describes each certificate, diploma or degree that you have earned and states its academic
equivalency in the United States.

Universities, below given steps will assist you to how to get the WES verification done.

2. Get your documents in order before contacting SLIIT. For more information, contact SLIIT on `011 754 3112`.
3. Submit all necessary documents and proof of applicable SLIIT fees.

## What is a "phishing" email or an attack?

What is a "phishing" email or an attack?
Phishing is an attack that attempts to steal your money, or your identity, by getting you to reveal personal
information -- such as credit card numbers, bank information, or passwords. This could come in the form as an
email or a text message with a clickable link or an attachment.
What to do if you receive a phishing email ?
If you find the email message or the text suspicious, do not open it or click any of the links or the attachments in
it.
Delete it and report to your IT services.
What to do after accidently responding to a phishing email?
The likelihood that a user might accidently respond to a phishing email is becoming inevitable.

1. Change account passwords
   Do this immediately, to prevent your account being hacked.
   After the attacker ties the phishing attack victim to a particular account, they will try to use similar credentials
   on the user’s other known accounts.
   So, it’s crucial to change passwords not only for the expected compromised account but also for other associated
   user accounts.
2. Report the phishing incident
   By reporting to relevant IT Services of your organization, you are keeping everyone aware that such an attack is
   taking place. Then others will be more cautious.
3. Investigate the phishing attack
   Well-timed reporting of an incident—that is, as soon as a user realizes they’ve responded to a phishing
   email—allows information security technical staff to launch crucial information-gathering about the attack.
4. Keep an eye on your account activity
   Make sure that after changing the passwords, no other activities have taken place.
   Contact SLIIT ITSD for more information.
